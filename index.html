What does this one do?

I'm a junior at Yale, pursuing an M.S./B.S. in mathematics and B.S. in physics.

Currently, I am an undergraduate researcher at the Perimeter Institute for Theoretical Physics in Waterloo, Canada. My research centers on finding ways around the curse of dimensionality, by applying methods called sparse grid discretizations to solving high-dimensional dynamical systems, such as Einstein's field equations, and working with large data sets.

During the year, I work as an undergraduate researcher in computational neuroscience at the Murray Lab, under Dr. John Murray at the Yale School of Medicine, Psychiatry N3 Division. My main work focuses on studying the dynamics that allow recurrent neural networks to form working memory for cognitive task function.

This website serves as a repository for some of my academic work, research, and personal projects. Look around and find something you like.
